	\title{Microneedle Biosensors for Measurement of Biomarkers for Sepsis and Modelling of Electron Transfer in Aptamer-based Biosensors}
	\begin{center}
	    \author{Binghuan Li, Safiyya Musa, Anastasia Sysoeva, Danielle Tan, James Tang, \\ Peter Xie}    
	\end{center}
\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
Sepsis is a life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection. Early sepsis diagnosis is vital to avoid delay in administering appropriate treatment, translating to increased survival rates \cite{teggert2020biomarkers}. Electrochemical microneedle (MN) biosensors are capable of minimally invasive, continuous sepsis biomarkers monitoring in skin interstitial fluid (ISF), facilitating early sepsis diagnosis. In this report, we studied three sepsis biomarkers: (1) nitrite, (2) hydrogen peroxide, and (3) lactate. Linear calibrations for nitrite were obtained using differential pulse voltammetry. Effects of albumin interference were also tested. Hydrogen peroxide (0 to 300$\mu$M concentrations) was measured using chronoamperometry. Linear calibrations obtained from coulometry showed a significant charge increase with increasing hydrogen peroxide concentration. Lactate was measured using amperometry with rotating-disk-electrode (RDE) instrumentation and lactate oxidase as the coating enzyme optimised our lactate concentrations detection range to cover healthy and septic levels. We present a modelling framework analysing chronoamperometric experiments performed on aptamer-based biosensors involving a novel numerical inverse Laplace (NIL) algorithm, capable of decomposing representative rate constants values ($k_{A}$ and $k_{AT}$) \cite{arroyo2018subsecond}.  Then, a physical coarse-grained model of the aptamer in its target bound and unbound configurations was created.
\end{abstract}
\end{@twocolumnfalse}]
